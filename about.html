About the Island

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano.

Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

Taniti at a Glance

Restaurants

Taniti currently has 10 restaurants: five serve mostly local fish and rice, three serve American-style meals, and two serve Pan-Asian cuisine.

Grocery Stores

Taniti has two supermarkets, two smaller grocery stores, and one convenience store that is open 24 hours a day.

Lodging

Taniti offers a wide range of lodging, from inexpensive hostels to a large four-star resort, along with family-owned hotels and bed & breakfasts. All accommodations are strictly regulated and regularly inspected.

Entertainment

Visitors can enjoy beaches, rainforest adventures, the volcano, museums, fishing tours, snorkeling, zip-lining, pubs, microbreweries, dance clubs, movies, helicopter rides, arcades, art galleries, bowling, and soon a nine-hole golf course, mainly around Merriton Landing.

Sightseeing

Most tourists explore Taniti City, enjoying native architecture, white sandy beaches, boat and bus tours, rainforest hikes, and visits to the active volcano.

Transportation

Visitors mainly arrive by air via a small airport, with occasional cruise ships docking in Yellow Leaf Bay. Airport expansion is underway for larger jets.

Ground Transportation

Public buses operate in Taniti City from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m., private buses serve the rest of the island, taxis are available, rental cars can be rented near the airport, and bikes with helmets can be rented. Taniti City is walkable, especially around Merriton Landing.
